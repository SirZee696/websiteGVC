Image Arithmetic Division

Divide one image by another pixel-by-pixel. Useful for normalization, illumination correction, and ratio-based analysis.

How Division Works

Image division computes a ratio of corresponding pixels. To keep values in the 0–255 range, the ratio is scaled by 255.

The Formula

Result(x,y) = 255 × ImageA(x,y) ÷ max(ImageB(x,y), ε)

• Operates per-channel (R, G, B)

• ε prevents division by zero (e.g., ε = 1)

• Results are clamped to the 0–255 range

Typical Uses

Illumination Correction: Divide by a shading image to flatten lighting.

Normalization: Ratio between channels or images for analysis.

Flat-Fielding: Correct sensor non-uniformities using a reference frame.

Mathematical Insights

Properties of Division

Non‑Commutative: A ÷ B ≠ B ÷ A (order matters)

Non‑Associative: (A ÷ B) ÷ C ≠ A ÷ (B ÷ C)

Identity: A ÷ 255 = A (white image acts like 1 after scaling)

Amplification: Small B values brighten A; large B values darken A.

Common Pitfalls

Division by Zero: Use ε (e.g., 1) to avoid infinite results and banding.

Noise Boost: Low denominators amplify noise; smooth or regularize B.

Color Space: Perform division in linear space; sRGB ratios can be misleading.

Clipping: Ratios may exceed 255; clamp or tone‑map to preserve detail.
