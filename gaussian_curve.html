The Gaussian Curver 1D form

Also known as the "bell curve," this function is fundamental to statistics, probability, and many image processing algorithms like Gaussian blur.

The Formula

G(x) = (1/(σ√2π)) * e-((x-μ)²/(2σ²))

Parameters

  • Mean (μ): Determines the center of the peak. It's the average value.
  • Standard Deviation (σ): Controls the "spread" or width of the curve. A smaller sigma results in a taller, narrower peak, while a larger sigma creates a shorter, wider curve.

The shaded areas represent one (darker) and two (lighter) standard deviations from the mean.

Properties

  • Symmetry: The curve is perfectly symmetric around its mean (μ). The mean, median, and mode are all equal.
  • Total Area: The total area under the curve is always equal to 1, which is why it's a probability distribution.
  • The 68-95-99.7 Rule: Roughly 68% of the data falls within one standard deviation (σ) of the mean, 95% within two, and 99.7% within three.
