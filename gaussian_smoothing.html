Gaussian Smoothing

A powerful blurring filter that uses a weighted average based on a Gaussian (bell curve) distribution.

How It Works

Gaussian smoothing is a convolution-based filter. It applies a kernel where the weights are not uniform; instead, they follow a Gaussian distribution. This means the center pixel gets the highest weight, and weights decrease for neighboring pixels as their distance from the center increases.

The Formula

G(x,y) = (1/(2πσ²)) * e-((x²+y²)/(2σ²))

  • σ (Sigma) is the standard deviation, controlling the blur amount.
  • The kernel is normalized so all weights sum to 1.

Common Uses

Noise Reduction: Very effective at reducing Gaussian noise.

Edge Detection Preprocessing: A key step in algorithms like Canny edge detection to reduce noise before finding edges.

Natural Blurring: Creates a smooth, natural-looking blur often used in photography and computer graphics.
