Robert Cross Detector

A simple, fast edge detector that approximates the image gradient using two 2x2 kernels.

How It Works

The Robert Cross operator uses two small convolution kernels to find differences across diagonal lines. The first kernel, Gx, detects edges at 45°, and the second, Gy, detects edges at 135°.

The Formula

Magnitude = √(Gx² + Gy²)

  • Gx and Gy are the results of convolving the image with the two kernels.
  • The magnitude gives the overall strength of the edge.
